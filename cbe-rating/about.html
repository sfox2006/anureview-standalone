About ANRevU

A student project built to make course choice fairer.

ANRevU is a student-focused project built to correct the information asymmetries that everyday students go through so that everyone can know what an academic or a course is actually like.

Browse ratings Data sources

1. Why this exists and what its purpose is

Official course descriptions and handbook pages only tell part of the story. Students still have to commit large amounts of time, money and energy without really knowing what a course will feel like in practice or what an academic is like to learn from.

ANRevU exists to reduce that gap. It gives students a place to share honest experiences, compare workloads, assessment design, interest level and teaching style, and make better-informed decisions before locking in a class.

If you are a student from another university and want to collaborate on adapting this model for your own campus, building a local version, or helping extend the broader project, reach out at ...@gmail.com.

2. How it is funded

ANRevU is paid for by students who care about making course choice easier and fairer for everyone else. It is not set up as a profit-making business and it is not designed to extract value from students.

The goal is simple: build something useful, keep it running, and give students better information than they currently have when making major academic decisions.

3. Why people need to help

This only works if students actually contribute. A review website is useful only when people leave honest feedback, because each review helps the next student make a better decision.

In other words, this is a student-helping-students project. If everyone shares a little, everyone is better off. If nobody contributes, the information gap stays exactly where it is.
